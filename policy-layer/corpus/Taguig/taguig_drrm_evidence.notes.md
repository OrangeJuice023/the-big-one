## Headline finding

Taguig is a **capable, on-fault LGU** (BGC-scale infrastructure; 12 barangays
on the WVF trace; 5-storey Center for Disaster Management in Brgy. Central
Signal) with a **portal-that-does-not-publish-statutory-documents** posture
similar to Marikina. Operational DRRM activity is well-documented on the LGU
portal; the underlying statutory register (creating ordinance, LDRRMP,
updated CLUP) is not. This puts Taguig in the same lapse cluster as Marikina
(`access-foi`) rather than with Pasig (`access-opaque`) or Pateros
(`access-none`).

## 1. DRRMO establishment (OB1)

- **Taguig City DRRMO** is a functioning department under the City Mayor.
  Named officers: Ronald Galicia (Officer-in-Charge, per 2011 taguig.gov.ph
  press release re: AusAID LIDAR project). Current chief unknown; not yet
  verified via DILG-NCR forum records like Pateros/Pasig.
- **Center for Disaster Management** — 5-storey building in Barangay Central
  Signal; specialized training rooms, evacuation centers, government offices
  co-located. (Source: taguig.gov.ph/peace-and-order/)
- **Named operational assets:** MOCCOV (Mobile Command and Control Vehicle,
  DOST partnership); Aerial Platform Fire Truck (70m articulating boom, 500kg
  lift, reaches 20–22-storey buildings — meaningful for BGC high-rises).
- **Partnerships documented:** BGC Estate Association (BGCEA) + American
  Battle Monuments Commission (ABMC) MOU for BGC-area disaster preparedness;
  MMDA training partnership.
- **Facebook page:** facebook.com/TaguigCityDRRMO/ — currently
  restricted/private view (activity evidence only if content accessible).

## 3. Hazard maps & risk assessment (OB4, OB5)

### GMMA READY / BRACE hazard maps
- Multi-Hazard and Risk Maps IEC Campaign at Taguig City University
  Auditorium, Dec 16–17 (year unspecified in search snippet; source
  taguig.gov.ph/taguig-steps-up-hazards-awareness/).
- Taguig was the **"pioneer" LGU for the multi-hazard IEC campaign** —
  Mayor Lani Cayetano quoted. ⚠️ "pioneer" is Taguig's own PIO framing;
  needs second-source verification if used externally.
- Program parents: **GMMA READY** (Enhancing Greater Metro Manila Areas
  Institutional Capacities for Effective Disaster/Climate Risk Management
  towards Sustainable Development) + **BRACE Project** (Building Resistance
  and Awareness of Metro Manila Communities to Natural Disaster and Climate
  Change Impacts). Regional program, not Taguig-specific.

### LIDAR 3D disaster map (2011, AusAID)
- AusAID selected Taguig as pilot testing site for LIDAR-based geo-hazard
  mapping under BRACE. Source: taguig.gov.ph/3-d-disaster-map-from-ausaid-
  to-lift-mayor-lanis-disaster-risk-reduction-efforts-in-taguig/ (also
  archived on OpenStreetMap talk-ph mailing list, 9 Oct 2011).
- Historical (2011); may or may not still be operational.

### WVF corridor barangays
- **"12 barangays reportedly situated over the West Valley Fault"** (source:
  taguig.gov.ph "Disaster Preparedness Training at Pinagsama Phase 2 Covered
  Court"). ⚠️ **Specific list of 12 barangays not enumerated in search
  snippet** — requires direct portal fetch or FOI to name them.
- Distinct from the 2012 flood corridor (see §6).

### CLUP-documented geology (OB5 context)
The Taguig CLUP 2000–2020 (see §5) documents the fault-system geology:
> The presence of the marine sediments suggests that the quaternary alluvium
> was probably deposited after uplift of the Guadalupe formation, possibly
> as a result of movements along the **Marikina Valley Fault System (MVFS)**
> (Punongbayan, 1996).

⚠️ The CLUP uses "Marikina Valley Fault System (MVFS)" — an older/alternate
name for what is now more commonly called the Valley Fault System (VFS) with
West Valley Fault and East Valley Fault segments. Same geological feature;
naming reflects a 1996 citation. Consistency check needed if cited externally.

## 4. Drills & operations (OB6)

- **Mayor Cayetano institutionalized regular earthquake drills** in city
  schools and public/private establishments. Source: taguig.gov.ph
  "Disaster Preparedness Training at Pinagsama Phase 2 Covered Court."
- **Mobile Earthquake Simulator** deployed for public education.
- MMDA co-conducted drills.
- Evidence: multiple documented drill events on the taguig.gov.ph news feed.

## 5. Land-use / DRR mainstreaming (OB8)

### Taguig CLUP + Zoning Ordinance 2000–2020

- **Adopted:** Sangguniang Panlungsod **Resolution No. 129, s. 2003** on
  19 May 2003.
- **Endorsed:** MMDA Resolution No. 03-21, dated 26 June 2003.
- **Approved:** HLURB Board Resolution R-744 s. 2003 (24 Sept 2003).
  Primary URL: `https://dhsud.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/Laws_Issuances/00_Archive/HLURB/Board_Resolutions/R-744%20s.%202003.pdf`
- **DRR provisions:** the CLUP/Zoning Ordinance includes:
  - **10-meter easement** along Pasig River banks (per Metro Manila Zoning
    Ordinance 81-01 + MMDA Resolution No. 3, s. 1993).
  - **5-meter easement on both sides of earthquake fault traces identified
    by PHIVOLCS.** ← identical rule to Makati Zoning Ord. 2012-102 §37 and
    Pasig City's 5m no-build buffer. **Three LGUs, same PHIVOLCS-derived
    fault-line rule.**
- **Primary CLUP text availability:**
  - **Only on 3rd-party academic mirrors** (Scribd, Studocu, PDFCoffee,
    Course Hero). Not hosted on taguig.gov.ph.
  - Same access pattern as Marikina CLUP 2018-2027.
- **Age concern:** the CLUP is 2000–2020 (expired). An updated CLUP
  post-2020 is FOI-gated (see §7).

## 8. Historic flood corridor (barangay-level exposure, context)

From the 2012 state-of-calamity article, flood-affected Taguig barangays
included: **Bagumbayan, Sta. Ana, New Lower Bicutan, Lower Bicutan, Ususan,
Hagonoy, San Miguel, Bambang, Tuktukan, Katuparan, Calzada-Tipas, Palingon,
Wawa, Western Bicutan, Napindan, Ligid-Tipas, Ibayo-Tipas, Fort Bonifacio.**
Even Taguig City Hall took ≥2 ft flooding.

⚠️ Note: **Fort Bonifacio flooded** in 2012 — worth flagging that even the
BGC-adjacent area isn't hazard-free despite its high-rise development.

The CLUP 2000-2020 documents the underlying flood-exposure driver: **≥70% of
Taguig lies below 12.5m elevation; 66% of area subject to Laguna Lake
overflow via Pasig River** (source: 3rd-party CLUP mirrors).
