# Pateros — DRRM evidence extract (the contrast LGU)

**Ingested:** S7. Pateros is the transparency-floor case.
**Headline finding:** DRRM activity in Pateros is real and ongoing, but the
**Municipality publishes no DRRM documents itself** — no official portal, no
consolidated public website. All evidence reaches us through **non-LGU
channels**: national news (PIA), a national transparency portal (SubayBAYAN),
and Facebook. This is an LGU-wide **`access`** finding, and it is the point of
the Pateros case: *does DRRM, doesn't document it publicly.*

Contrast placement: QC (public 8/8, 0 lapses) → Makati (public, 2 access-lapses)
→ Marikina (partial) → Pasig (FOI-gated) → **Pateros (no portal; Facebook-only).**

- Project: "Procurement of DRRM-Pateros Rescue Equipment and Personal Gears."
  Program: Disaster Recovery and Rehabilitation Assistance Program.
  Project Code: DRRAP-21-13-76-06-000-1.

## S7b — firming addendum (revised findings)

Two claims from the S7a extract above were wrong and need correction. The
firming pass materially strengthens the OB1 call and reframes the headline.

### Correction 1: pateros.gov.ph DOES exist
Prior claim "no official portal" was wrong. **pateros.gov.ph is live** (WordPress).
However:
- Front page is stale (references 2015 census, Jan-2022 permit schedule, a
  2020 COVID ordinance).
- Hosts a **RESOLUTION-1988-2019.pdf** index (ordinances/resolutions sponsored
  by Vice Mayor Capco et al., through 2019) — indexed content is mostly pre-2000,
  no DRRM-specific ordinance visible in retrievable snippets.
- Hosts a **Citizen's Charter 2022 (2nd Edition)** — references only Ord.
  2004-020 (Revenue Code); no DRRM ordinance surfaced.
- Direct fetch of the resolutions index PDF blocked by bot-detection (need
  manual browser download for full text).
- Refined finding: portal **exists but does not publish DRRM content.**
  This is still an `access` pattern (stale/minimal portal, DRRM docs not
  reachable), not "no portal at all."

### Correction 2: regulator-level recognition upgrades OB1
Two NEW primary/near-primary sources (both government) name Pateros's LDRRMO
publicly and by officer:

### Correction 3: OB7 has a specific 2013 state-of-calamity anchor
- Inquirer (20 Aug 2013): "Pateros placed under state of calamity" — the
  **Pateros municipal council issued a resolution** declaring the state of
  calamity and **releasing a portion of the calamity fund**; Mayor Joey Medina
  confirmed. → newsinfo.inquirer.net/470281/…
- Confirms: a **calamity fund exists** and the council **actually taps it via
  resolution** (per RA 10121 §21). The resolution itself is not on the portal
  → OB7 stays partial (access), evidence strengthened.

### Headline (revised)

**Previous:** "Pateros has no portal; DRRM only visible via Facebook + national channels."
**Corrected:** "Pateros has a portal, but the portal is stale and does not
publish DRRM. The LDRRMO exists and is regulator-attested (DILG-NCR, OCD-NCR),
tapping the calamity fund via council resolution, but the underlying statutory
documents (creating ordinance, LDRRMP, hazard maps, CDP DRR chapters,
appropriation) are not on the portal."

The **transparency gradient still holds**, and the corrected version is
actually a *stronger* finding: Pateros isn't a no-portal LGU, it's a **portal-that-does-not-publish-DRRM** LGU. That's a documented editorial choice by the LGU (or its portal maintainer), not a resource-absence excuse.
