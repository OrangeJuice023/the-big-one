## The correction

The S6 handoff (and my S7a work) treated the Annual Report 2022 as a
prospective source of **OB6 (drills) activity evidence** — Makati's yearly
report presumed to document the DRRMO's drill program alongside other
activities. Direct inspection shows this assumption was wrong.

**The Annual Report 2022 does NOT contain any of the following:**
West Valley Fault; Valley Fault System; earthquake scenario; earthquake
drills; shake drills; PHIVOLCS; NDRRMC; DRRM Office; Disaster Risk Reduction;
seismic hazard; liquefaction; ground shaking; earthquake preparedness;
building vulnerability; evacuation planning.

It focuses on **environmental / climate resilience** rather than seismic /
disaster preparedness. Makati's yearly reporting posture in 2022 emphasized
climate, not earthquake DRRM.

## Two secondary findings from the inspection

The inspection did surface useful content — just for different obligations
than OB6:

### 1. Makati State of Climate Emergency (2022)
- Makati declared a **State of Climate Emergency in 2022.**
- ⚠️ The enacting instrument (resolution number, ordinance number, or EO
  number) not identified in the inspection notes; needs verification of the
  specific legal instrument before external citation. Provenance TODO.
- Governance-layer signal — not DRR-specific but supports OB8 mainstreaming
  argument.

### 2. Climate program budget allocation
- **17% of Makati's 2022 Annual Investment Program (AIP) allocated to
  climate-related programs and projects.**
- Complements OB7 evidence at the policy-signal level (Makati is putting real
  money behind climate resilience — though the 5% LDRRMF + 30% QRF specific
  is a distinct RA 10121 §21 requirement, not this AIP allocation).

### 3. Long-term planning updates listed
The report references updates to:
- Solid Waste Management plan
- Water Quality Management plan
- Local Climate Change Action Plan (LCCAP)
- Greenhouse Gas Inventory
- Environmental Planning
- GIS Mapping / Vegetation Mapping / green spaces / environmental monitoring
- Air quality monitoring / plastic monitoring / waste management

None of these are DRRM-specific, but **GIS capability** is a substrate for
hazard mapping, exposure analysis, evacuation planning, and EQ risk
modeling. That's a supporting infrastructure finding for OB5 (Makati has the
technical capacity to do risk assessment) rather than direct assessment
evidence.

## `access-opaque` empirical confirmation

The Annual Report 2022 is a **scanned PDF with no text layer** — full stop.
Even a citizen who finds the file cannot search it with Ctrl-F, cannot get
indexed by search engines, cannot extract text for use in accessibility
tools. The user could only inspect it via OCR. This is a textbook
`access-opaque` case:

- File is published (LGU did upload it).
- File is not `access-broken` (link works, file opens).
- File is not `access-foi` (no request needed).
- File is not `access-none` (not missing).
- File IS `access-opaque`: technically online, functionally unsearchable.

**Empirical support for the sub-type.** Would apply here if we were counting
the AR2022 toward any obligation — but since it's content-negative for OB6,
it doesn't downgrade a status. Method-level finding only.
