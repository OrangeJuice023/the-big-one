## Why this document matters (and its limits)
- It is a **2008 secondary case study**, so everything below **pre-dates RA 10121
  (2010)**. Treat as **historical context / corroboration**, not as evidence of
  *current-law* compliance. It does **not** substitute for collecting Marikina's
  post-2010 LDRRMP or DRRMO-creating ordinance.
- Its strongest contribution to our scorecard is the **earthquake** angle: it
  documents a Marikina *Comprehensive Earthquake Disaster Reduction program* from
  2005 — five years before RA 10121 — which corroborates OB5 (risk assessment /
  seismic sub-lens) and the narrative that Marikina has a long DRR track record.

## Marikina DRRM ordinance timeline (as reported by the case study)
- **Ord. 59, s. 1993** — keep sidewalks/alleys/public spaces clear (movement
  during emergencies).
- **Ord. 10, s. 1994** — declares a 96 m easement each side of the Marikina River
  centerline; authorizes relocation of residents within it. (Basis of the
  "Save the Marikina River" resettlement.)
- **Ord. 264, s. 1998** — creates a **Disaster Management Office ("Rescue 161")**:
  emergency preparedness, response coordination, first-response training,
  monitoring; defines staffing, salaries, annual budget. → OB1/OB3 **historical
  context only** (pre-RA 10121 office; superseded by the post-2010 LDRRMO regime).
- **Ord. 171, s. 1999** — declares two riverside lots a danger zone / non-buildable.
- **Ord. 54, s. 2005** — authorizes use of **20% of the Calamity Fund** for
  disaster preparedness (tools/equipment). → OB7 **context** (pre-RA 10121; RA
  10121 later sets the 5% LDRRMF / 30% QRF regime — different basis).
- **Ord. 109, s. 2005** — creates the **Comprehensive Earthquake Disaster
  Reduction program and action plan**; identifies the roles of **20 offices/
  agencies**. → OB5 **corroboration** (seismic sub-lens); strongest single item here.

## Preparedness / activity evidence (dated 2008 snapshot)
- **Rescue 161** emergency operations center (per Ord. 264 s.1998); reported 2004
  response times ~3–4 min; DILG *Galing Pook* Hall of Fame (1999); WHO "healthy
  city" citation (2006). → OB6 activity **context** (dated).
- Investments tied to **earthquake risk**: pontoon bridges, Collapsed Building
  Retrieval Boxes, heavy/light rescue equipment, narrowcast emergency radio
  (DZBF-1674 MHz). → OB5/OB6 context.
- Published a **Disaster Management Handbook** distributed to HOA presidents and
  other LGUs.
- Marikina lies **along the Valley Fault Line** and faces riverine flooding,
  fault earthquakes, and liquefaction (case study, Introduction) — direct tie to
  our WVF loss-model corridor.

## Flood-exposure figures (case study Table 3; WMO/Marikina 2007)
| item | 1992 flood | 2004 flood |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| land flooded | 6.36 km² | 4.40 km² |
| residences exposed | 10,446 | 4,789 |
| businesses exposed | 450 | 289 |
| critical facilities | 11 | 9 |

Climate Type I; annual rainfall 1,700–3,200 mm; ~80% May–Oct. 1992 event flooded
~27.5% of the city (~6.4 km²). (Useful independent cross-check for flood-exposure
context; our loss model is seismic, so this is corroborative background, not input.)
