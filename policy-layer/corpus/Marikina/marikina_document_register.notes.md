## Policy finding: the eFOI-gated pattern is a national compliance gap

The pattern for Marikina is uniform and deliberate: **documents exist, are
active, and are shared upon formal application — but the LGU does not maintain
a public digital repository.** This isn't a data-collection nuisance; it's a
substantive compliance issue against the spirit (and arguably the letter) of
RA 10121.

- **RA 10121 IRR Rule 6 §7 and §12(c)(10)** require hazard maps and DRRM plans
  to be **"publicly displayed."**
- **RA 10121 §11(b)(1)** requires the LDRRMP to be **formulated and tested.**
  Testing implies drills; drills imply community awareness; community awareness
  requires a plan the community can read without applying for it.
- **FOI (EO 2, s. 2016)** was designed for records **not otherwise proactively
  disclosed** — a residual channel. RA 10121-mandated documents are supposed to
  be in the proactive-disclosure category, not the FOI-request category.

**The national policy gap:** RA 10121 says "publicly displayed" but does not
specify *online / permanently downloadable / proactively disclosed*. EO 2 then
makes eFOI the default fallback for anything not proactively disclosed.
Result: an LGU can technically satisfy "publicly displayed" by keeping a hard
copy at city hall and granting eFOI on request, without ever putting the PDF
on its website. Marikina's posture — active eFOI compliance, no proactive
digital disclosure — is that gap in operation.

This distinguishes Marikina from Makati (access-broken: intended to publish,
link failed) and from Pateros (access-none: never attempted digital
disclosure). All three fail "publicly displayed," but for different reasons —
which is why the ledger now uses `access-broken` / `access-foi` /
`access-none` as sub-types.
