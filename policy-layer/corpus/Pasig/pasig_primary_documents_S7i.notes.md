**Additional detail from the primary text (not in the compiled summary):**

- Statutory basis cited on the face of the resolution: **RA 10121
  §12(c)(6)** and the **IRR Rule 6, Section 4(7)**.
- **A predecessor plan existed: the 2017–2022 Pasig DRRM Plan.** The
  resolution records that it "was revisited and updated by the City Disaster
  Risk Reduction and Management Council and found to be satisfactory and
  sufficient." This establishes an **update cycle**, which is the §12(c)(6)
  obligation — previously we could only infer it.
- PCDRRMC Resolution No. 1 was issued **22 May 2023**; Sanggunian adoption
  followed 19 June 2023.
- Signed: Mayor **Victor Ma. Regis N. Sotto**; Vice-Mayor **Robert Vincent
  Jude B. Jaworski, Jr.** (presiding); Majority Floor Leader Mark Gil M.
  Delos Santos; attested Atty. Nicolo Joel B. Gutierrez, Acting City Council
  Secretary.

### 1b. BONUS — Committee on Disaster Resilience report (attached, pp. 4–9)

The PDF includes the **Committee Report** from the hearing that produced the
resolution. This is unusually rich and is the most detailed primary evidence
in the corpus for any LGU's plan-making process.

- **Date:** 7 June 2023, 4:00–5:00 PM, Gallery Session Hall, 7th Floor.
- **Agenda:** Proposed Resolution No. 271-2023 (became Res. 130-11).
- **Attendance:** Coun. Noel L. Agustin (Chairman), Coun. Raymund Francis S.
  Rustia (Vice Chairman), Couns. Delos Santos, Martires, Santiago, De Leon;
  **Mr. Bryant Q. Wong (DRRMO Chief)**; **EnP. Priscella B. Mejillano (City
  Planning Office)**; Ms. Cathy Vidar (Consultant); Ms. Joy Sarmiento (City
  Budget Office); Mr. Alvin Jalla and Mr. John Kenneth Agapito (PDRRMO).

**Substantive content:**

- **The LDRRMP is approximately 300 pages**, the updated version of 2022,
  reformulated by PCDRRM Council for a 6-year horizon.
- **Target confirmed from the primary record:** the consultant "highlighted
  that not only Pasig should be ready for the 7.2 magnitude or the Big One,
  but the 'preparedness' should engage all partners in the detailed
  planning… and aim for **zero casualties = zero death**," identifying public
  schools, ISF temporary shelters, and flood-prone residents as the
  populations at issue. ✅ This confirms the compiled summary's "zero
  casualties at M7.2" claim from the primary record.
- **OB8 confirmed from primary record:** DRRMO Chief Wong "emphasized that
  the DRRM Plan always linked to the broader plans of the City, namely the
  **CLWUP and the CDP** and to the national priorities set in the **NDRRMP**,
  and horizontally aligned with other sectoral and thematic plans of the LGU."
  Note this names **CLWUP** (the 2023–2031 instrument), corroborating the
  S7e discovery.
- **Plan structure (four parts):** (1) *Risk Profile*, drawn from the
  **Climate and Disaster Risk Assessment (CDRA)** following **DHSUD**
  guidelines plus risk assessments commissioned by PCDRRMO; (2) *Situational
  Analysis* across the four DRRM thematic areas with a **SWOC** analysis;
  (3) *Monitoring and evaluation*, periodic review; (4) *Execution* —
  partnership building, communications and feedback, resource mobilisation.
- **Budget linkage:** programmes and activities "were already identified,
  discussed and included in the recent investment hearing of **DRRMO 2024
  AIP** with proposed budget appropriation." → supports OB7.
- **Water filtration confirmed:** 4 units, "can create thousands of liters,"
  can purify to roughly salt-water level but needs many filters; **no site
  yet for the District 1 machine.** Ilog Pasig proposed as potable source
  given only one dam serves ~22M people.
- **Inter-LGU mutual aid:** Wong noted **Marikina willing to help** on the
  flood contingency plan, with assistance also available from Region 2 and
  from NGOs by land or sea.
- **Candid limitation on the record:** a discussion of building stock and
  retrofitting concluded that for a substantial share of affected buildings
  "retrofitting no longer viable but instead rebuilding them."
- ISF (informal settler families) cannot simply be evicted pre-event —
  UDHA Law — creating a documented preparedness constraint pending public
  and socialised housing.

### 1c. ⚠️ MAJOR FINDING — the council itself ordered the plan published, and it isn't

The Committee Report closes with this direction:

> "Lastly, the DRRMO and City Planning Office were reminded about the E-Copy
> of Pasig City Local Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Plan for
> 2023-2028, be provided to City Councilors for review as well as Zoning Map
> of risk prone and critical areas and be shared to other departments in
> preparation and readiness for the Big One. Provided further, for effective
> dissemination of information down to barangay /constituents, **copies be
> also available to them and down loadable thru Pasig City website/platform.**"

This is a **self-imposed, documented, on-the-record disclosure obligation**:
the Sangguniang Panlungsod's own Committee on Disaster Resilience directed
that the LDRRMP 2023–2028 be made downloadable from the Pasig City website
for barangays and constituents.

**The plan document has not been located on the Pasig City website.** The
*adopting resolution* (this document) is retrievable from
`assets.pasigcity.gov.ph`; the ~300-page *plan itself* is not, on the
evidence available.

Why this matters for the thesis: every other disclosure lapse in this corpus
is measured against an **external** standard — RA 10121's "publicly
displayed." This one is measured against the LGU's **own** recorded
instruction. It is not an outside critic saying the plan should be online; it
is the city council saying so, in the same document that adopted the plan.

⚠️ **PENDING — do not treat as established.** Absence from search is not
proof of absence from the website. Required before this is cited or before
any status changes: a **direct check of pasigcity.gov.ph** (browser, since
the site is bot-blocked) for the LDRRMP 2023–2028 full text, and a **Wayback
capture** of the result. See TODO below. If confirmed absent, **OB4 should
move from `present` to `partial`** — its obligation text is "hazard maps
**& plans** PUBLICLY displayed," and Pasig would then satisfy the maps limb
(physical WVF markers, verified S7h) but not the plans limb.

## 3. ❌ "Ordinance No. 08-08, Series of 2016" — NOT VERIFIED

`corpus/Pasig/pasig_ord_08_s2016_incentive_awards_NOT_contingency_plan.pdf`
— 3 pp, scan-only.

The file retrieved under this identifier is **Ordinance No. 08, Series of
2016**, and its subject matter is **entirely unrelated to disaster
management**:

> "AN ORDINANCE AMENDING ORDINANCE NO. 26, SERIES OF 2007, ENTITLED 'AN
> ORDINANCE MANDATING THE ANNUAL GRANT OF INCENTIVE AWARD TO INCOME
> GENERATING EMPLOYEES OR OFFICES WHICH HAVE EXCEEDED THE ASSIGNED REVENUE
> TARGETS PROVIDED FOR UNDER THE ANNUAL APPROPRIATING ACT', AND FOR OTHER
> PURPOSES."

It appropriates ₱1,000,000 annually for employee incentives and awards.
Authored by Councilors Gregorio P. Rupisan, Jr. and **Wilfredo F. Sityar**;
approved 1 September 2016; Mayor Robert C. Eusebio.

**The compiled summary claimed** that "Ordinance No. 08-08 (Series of 2016)"
was *"An Ordinance Adopting the Pasig City Earthquake and Flood Contingency
Plan for CY 2016–2021,"* authored by Councilors Richard C. Eusebio and
Wilfredo F. Sityar, and based on MMEIRS.

**What we can and cannot conclude:**

- The retrieved document is **not** the earthquake/flood contingency plan
  ordinance. That much is certain.
- Partial overlap exists — **Wilfredo F. Sityar authored both** the claimed
  document and the actual Ord. 08 s.2016 — so the compiled summary was not
  drawing on nothing.
- **The identifier may be the problem, not the underlying claim.** Pasig
  resolutions use a **two-part numbering format** (`130-11`, `269-11`), so
  "08-08" may be a genuine and distinct ordinance identifier rather than a
  typo for "08." If so, the correct document simply has not been retrieved yet.
- Alternatively the compiled summary conflated documents, as it appears to
  have done for Marikina's "Ord. 132 s.2011."

**Status:** the 2016 Earthquake and Flood Contingency Plan remains
**unverified**. Do not cite it. It is not currently load-bearing — OB2 and
OB6 are carried by Res. 130-11 and Res. 269-11, both now verified from
primary text — so this is a completeness gap rather than a hole in the
argument.

**This is the second compiled-summary claim to fail primary verification**
(after Marikina Ord. 132 s.2011). Both failures were caught because the claim
was flagged `PENDING` at ingest rather than recorded as fact. See
`ATTRIBUTION.md`.
