## Overhaul: Pasig moves off the FOI-gated floor

Prior state (S6/S7): Pasig was "FOI-gated — nothing surfaced." This turns out to
be materially wrong. Pasig has a **substantial, publicly-published DRRM
legislative register** — the docs were there; earlier searches just didn't
find them because of a *discoverability lapse* (see §"File-naming / scanned
PDFs" below), not an *access lapse*. This is the fifth reversal-of-priors
across S6–S7d (Pateros portal-exists correction; regulator recognition of
Pateros LDRRMO; Marikina Ord. 132 s.2011 identification; Marikina eFOI-gated
LDRRMP/CLUP; and now Pasig).

## 1. DRRMO establishment & operational mandate

### Ordinance No. 02, Series of 2015 — creates PCDRRMO
- **Author:** Councilor Wilfredo F. Sityar (+ co-authors).
- **Approved:** 5 February 2015.
- **Function:** formally established the Pasig City Disaster Risk Reduction
  and Management Office (PCDRRMO) as a **distinct department under the Office
  of the City Mayor.**
- **Repealing clause:** repealed **Ordinance No. 18, Series of 2010**
  (predecessor DRRM instrument — pre-current-law consolidation).
- **Scope:** defines **26 primary functions** including risk profiling,
  multi-hazard early warning systems, 24/7 disaster monitoring, and
  supervision of local emergency response forces.
- **Plantilla established:**
  | Position | Salary Grade | Mandate |
  |---|---|---|
  | Local DRRM Officer IV | SG 22 | Department Executive / Leadership |
  | Local DRRM Officer III | SG 18 | Administration & Training; Research & Planning; Operations & Warning (3 units) |
  | Local DRRM Officer II | SG 15 | RED Center Administration; Search & Rescue; Fire Brigade; Network/IT Administration |
  | Local DRRM Officer I | SG 11 | External Affairs; Relief Operations |
- **Initial appropriation:** **₱4,370,188.44** — personnel salaries, allowances,
  benefit contributions.
- **Current Department Chief:** Bryant Q. Wong, RN, EMT, MPM.
  *Cross-link:* Bryant Wong appeared as a panelist at the **DILG-NCR Local DRRMO
  Forum, 14 July 2026** alongside Marko S. Pielago (Pateros) — same regulator
  attestation logged in the Pateros note.

**Obligations discharged:** OB1 (established), OB3 (creating ordinance with
staff + budget).

## 2. Long-term DRRM plans

### A. Local DRRM Plan 2023–2028 (Resolution No. 130-11, Series of 2023)
- **Authors:** Councilors Noel L. Agustin, Raymund Francis S. Rustia.
- **Approved:** 19 June 2023.
- **Scope:** adopts **PCDRRMC Resolution No. 1, s.2023**, approving the 6-year
  Pasig City Local Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Plan (LDRRMP)
  2023–2028.
- **Target:** **zero casualties during a Magnitude 7.2 earthquake ("The Big
  One").** ← direct cross-link to the loss model's scenario.
- **Integration:** directly integrates DRR into CLUP and CDP; aligned to the
  National DRRM Framework.
- **Notable assets:** 4 mobile water filtration units (river/salt → potable).

**Obligations discharged:** OB2 (plan formulated & adopted), OB8 (mainstreamed
into CLUP/CDP explicitly).

### B. Earthquake Contingency Plan 2024–2026 (Resolution No. 269-11, Series of 2023)
- **Authors:** Councilors Rustia, Agustin.
- **Approved:** 6 December 2023.
- **Scope:** adopts **PCDRRMC Resolution No. 9, s.2023**; operational City
  Contingency Plan for Earthquakes 2024–2026.
- **Legal alignment:** explicitly compliant with **RA 10121** and RA 7160
  (Local Government Code 1991).
- **Nature:** "living, dynamic operational framework subject to continuous
  simulation, periodic testing, and regular updates" ← evidence of the RA
  10121 §11(b)(1) "tested" requirement.

**Obligations discharged:** OB2 (contingency layer of the plan), OB6 (testing regime).

### C. Historical: Ordinance No. 08-08, Series of 2016 — First EQ+Flood Contingency Plan 2016–2021
- **Title:** "An Ordinance Adopting the Pasig City Earthquake and Flood
  Contingency Plan for CY 2016–2021."
- **Authors:** Councilors Richard C. Eusebio, Wilfredo F. Sityar; co-authored
  by the full City Council.
- **Scientific foundation:** **based on the Metro Manila Earthquake Impact
  Reduction Study (MMEIRS)** modeling M7.2 WVF — **same basis as the loss
  model + QCDRRMP + Makati CDP.** Fourth statutory cross-corroboration.
- **Mandates:** scenario-based operational protocols for compound disasters
  (severe shaking + flood-wall/dike failures along Marikina & Pasig rivers);
  regular multi-sectoral EQ drills, evacuation simulations, multi-agency
  command structures.
- **Superseded by** Res. 269-11 s.2023 (Item B above), but establishes the
  historical spine for OB6.

**Obligations discharged:** OB2 (historical), OB6 (drills mandated).

## 3. Land-use / structural resilience

### Ordinance No. 06, Series of 2016 — Green Building Ordinance
- **Objective:** structural/eco design standards for high-density development.
- **Applicability:** mandatory for PUDs & new buildings > 10,000 sq.m; existing
  buildings > 10,000 sq.m subject to retrofitting within a 2-year grace period.
- **Certification:** minimum BERDE (Building for Ecologically Responsive
  Design Excellence) rating required.
- **Institutional setup:** **Green Building Office (GBO)** created under the
  Office of the City Building Official (OCBO).

**Obligations discharged:** OB8 (structural resilience mainstreamed).

## 4. Hazard identification & public display

### West Valley Fault profile
- Mechanism: ~100-km active dextral strike-slip fault; slip 10–12 mm/yr.
- Potential: **M7.2–7.6 earthquake ("The Big One").**
- **Directly-affected Pasig barangays:** Ugong, Bagong Ilog, San Joaquin,
  Kalawaan, Kapitolyo, Manggahan.
- **Liquefaction high-risk zones (PEIS VIII+):** Ugong, Kapitolyo, Manggahan
  — unconsolidated alluvium + deltaic sands from Pasig/Marikina basins.

### 5-meter no-build buffer zone (WVF trace)
Pasig enforces a **5-meter no-build buffer on both sides of the WVF trace**
(10-meter total restricted corridor) per PHIVOLCS + National Building Code.
*Cross-link:* **identical rule** to Makati Zoning Ord. 2012-102 §30 (WVF→OPN)
and §37 (5-m PHIVOLCS-certified fault easement). Same standard, two LGUs.

### Joint PHIVOLCS Marker Project
> Pasig was **one of only three Metro Manila LGUs (alongside Quezon City and
> Makati)** that actively installed **physical ground markers, signs, and
> paint lines** along public roads and borders marking the exact WVF trace.

This is **literal public display of hazard identification** per RA 10121 IRR
Rule 6 §7 — arguably stronger than any of the other four LGUs' publication
approach, because it doesn't depend on citizens visiting a portal at all.

**Obligations discharged:** OB4 (physically publicly displayed — upgrade
above and beyond hazard tiles), OB5 (documented risk assessment with named
barangays + liquefaction zones).

## 5. Operational mitigation framework

- **Structural audits (OCBO):** routine on multi-story commercial (Ortigas
  Center segment), residential complexes, public schools near the fault line.
- **Urban planning (CLUP + CDP):** PHIVOLCS hazard maps directly integrated;
  restricts high-density zoning on soft-soil/fault zones.
- **Evacuation staging:** Pasig City Sports Complex, open park spaces,
  non-fault barangay grounds pre-designated for immediate post-EQ triage.
- **Water self-sufficiency:** 4 mobile water filtration units.

## 6. Citizen's Charter services (activity evidence)

- **Research & Planning:** provides copies of DRRM plans, hazard maps,
  technical risk documents on formal request (free; USB drive required).
  Reviews Barangay DRRM Fund Investment Plans. Provides technical evaluators
  for EQ/fire evacuation drills at schools, private entities, barangays.
- **Training:** USAR, water rescue, HazMat, firefighting, BLS, First Aid,
  MCI management. Tiered fees (Pasig residents pay ~half of non-residents).
- **Warning / C3:** 24/7 hotlines, CCTV operations, warning-system
  maintenance.
- **Operations:** free pre-hospital trauma care in-city; K9, drones, fire
  suppression teams; event standby.

**Obligations discharged:** OB6 (drill evaluation services + training program
— publicly documented in the Citizen's Charter).

## 7. LDRRM Fund — status

Ord. 02 s.2015 established the initial appropriation of ₱4,370,188.44 for
personnel/allowances/benefits. However, RA 10121 §21 requires **5% of local
revenue as LDRRMF, with 30% reserved as QRF** — an annual determination. The
plantilla appropriation confirms the fund exists and is being spent, but does
not by itself verify the 5% + 30% structure for the current fiscal year.

**Obligation:** OB7 partial — appropriation confirmed at establishment;
current-year 5%/30% budget resolution not in corpus.

## File-naming / scanned-PDF finding (discoverability lapse)

**The reason earlier searches missed the Pasig register:** the PDFs are online,
but the discoverability layer is broken. Two overlapping issues:

1. **Opaque filenames.** Files hosted as `ordinance.pdf`,
   `RESOLUTION-1988-2019.pdf`, `resolution 130-11.pdf` — no descriptive title,
   no ordinance-number in the URL path, no metadata index. A citizen searching
   for "Pasig DRRMO ordinance" hits no signal from the LGU domain. Search
   engines can't rank pages that don't declare what they are.
2. **Scanned PDFs with no text layer.** Even when a citizen finds the file, it
   can't be searched with Ctrl-F, indexed by full-text search, or excerpted
   without manual OCR. Native-PDF export from the same word processor would
   solve this at zero incremental cost. (Same problem blocks the Makati
   Annual Report 2022 for OB6.)

**Policy characterization.** This is neither `access-broken` (link works, file
opens), `access-foi` (no request needed — the file is public), nor
`access-none` (LGU did upload it). It's a **fourth mode of failing "publicly
displayed"**: the file is technically online, but the discoverability and
searchability necessary for the document to *function as* a public disclosure
are missing.

**Provisional name:** `access-opaque` — file online but unindexable,
unfindable, and/or unsearchable due to naming, format, or missing metadata.

**Relationship to compliance.** RA 10121 §11(b)(1) requires the plan to be
"formulated and tested," which presupposes public awareness. A publication
that no one can find or search does not, in practice, produce public
awareness. Ledger note: `access-opaque` is a *softer* lapse than
`access-broken`/`access-foi`/`access-none` — the LGU IS publishing, just
badly. A conservative call is to leave Pasig's obligations at `present`
(because the docs ARE public if you can find them) and log `access-opaque`
as a *methodology finding* rather than a status-capping lapse. Ask before
formalizing.

**Cross-cutting.** This is a general finding across all five LGUs, not
Pasig-specific. Makati's `ordinance.pdf` filename is the same pattern.
Makati's Annual Report 2022 (scanned) is the same pattern. Marikina's
`RESOLUTION-1988-2019.pdf` is the same pattern.

## The transparency gradient (updated, S7d)

| LGU | Signature | Present |
|---|---|---|
| QC | proactive digital disclosure | 8/8 |
| **Pasig** | **published-but-discoverability-lapse** (opaque names, scanned) | **7/8** |
| Makati | attempted digital disclosure, links broken | 5/8 |
| Marikina | deliberate eFOI-only channel | 3/8 present, 5 partial (access-foi) |
| Pateros | never attempted digital disclosure | 1/8 present |

**Revised thesis:** the divergence across LGUs is about **transparency
posture**, not the existence of DRRM. Every LGU on this list does DRRM, and
each fails "publicly displayed" for a *distinct* reason. Pasig moves from
"floor" to nearly-top, and the story that replaces the earlier
FOI-gated framing is:

> Pasig has a mature, publicly-adopted DRRM register that is nearly on par
> with QC's — the transparency gap is discoverability, not disclosure.
