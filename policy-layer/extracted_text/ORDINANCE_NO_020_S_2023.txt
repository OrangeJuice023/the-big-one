WHEREAS, studies made by Ana-Lisa Orallo Deputy Chief of the Report Development Section
in the operations Division of the Office of the Civil Defense disclosed that among all natural
incidents, earthquake was recorded as the second most deadly and damaging calamity in the
country and, on record, over the past four decades, earthquakes have spawned an accumulated
value of 20,701 casualties, 1,808,889 persons affected and 69.74 million pesos worth of damage
in properties;

WHEREAS, according to a study conducted by Japan Intemational Cooperation Agency (2004),
a 7.2-magnitude earthquake tagged as the "Big One" in the West Valley Fault, which is ripe for
movement, could kill up to 34,000 people and possibly injure 100,000 others due to collapsed
buildings in Metro Manila and neighboring provinces;

WHEREAS, the Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS) reports that
this West Valley Fault extends to 129.47 kilometers from Dofla Remedios Trinidad in Bulacan,
passing through San Jose del Monte City, and Norzagaray in Bulacan then traverses to cities in
Metro Manila including Quezon City, Marikina, Pasig, Makati, Taguig, and Muntinlupa;

WHEREAS, just recently, on February 6, a massive 7.S-magnitude eartlquake devastated
southeastem Turkey and parts of Syri4 and claimed at least 40,000 lives, and on February l, a
magnitude 6.0 earthquake shook New Bataan Town in Davao de Oro, and in the moming of

WHEREAS, nobody can tell when there will be an earthquake even the ,,Big One,', but
everyone more importantly the govemment can take steps to anticipate, reducJ the risk of
damage, injuries, or loss of life by increasing the level of preparedniss of the people on the
tireats and impacts ofearthquakes; and

WHf,REAS, this Ordinance seeks to enhance the preparedness of residents and establishments
by institutionalizing an earthquake awareness and safety drills program developed and initiated
by the City Government of Marikina in coordination with relevant national govemment agencies.

SECTION 1. Title.- This Ordinance shall be known and cited as the "Marikina City Earthquake
Preparedness Ordinance of 2023"

SECTION 2. Declaration of Policy.- It is hereby declared policy of the City Government of
Marikina to increase the level of preparedness of the citizens and establishments about
earthquakes, reduce the risk of injury and death through the institutionalization of an earthquake
awareness and safety drills program.

SECTION 3. Coverage-- This Ordinance shall apply to all residents of barangays and
subdivisions, employees of public and private establishments which include but not limited to
govemment agencies, public and private schools, business stores and shopping malls, cinemas,
and restaurants.

SECTION 4. Development and Inili ion of an Eorlhquake Awareness and Safety Drills
Program. - The Marikina City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Offrce (MCDRRMO)
in consultation with relevant local and national govemment oflices and/or agencies shall develop
and regularly initiate an earthquake awareness and safety drills program aimed at enhancing
preparedness ofresidents and establishments operating in the City. Such program shall form part
of the MCDRRMO annual plan and budget.

Annual report of the program shall be submitted to the Office of the City Mayor and the City
Council for purposes of evaluation and necessary support or enactment of legislation as the case
maybe.

SECTION 5. Implementing Rules and Regulations.- The Marikina City Disaster Risk
Reduction Council, Liga ng mga Barangay, OIfice of Public Safety and Security, Business
Permit and Licensing Offrce, Marikina PNP Station, Marikina BFP Station, and other relevant
government office or agency, shall issue the appropriate implementing rules and regulations,
policy and guidelines in the enforcement and implementation of this Ordinance.

SECTION 6. Repealing Clause.- All local ordinances, rules and regulations, or parts thereof
that are inconsistent with the provisions of this Ordinance are hereby repealed or modified
accordingly.

SECTION 7. Separability Clause.- The provisions of this Ordinance are hereby declared to be
separable and, if any clause, sentence or provision or section of this ordinance or application
thereof to any person or ctcumstances should for any reason be held invalid by a competent
court, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or application ofthe Ordinance.

SECTION 8. Effectivity Claase.- This Ordinance shall take effect upon approval. _
